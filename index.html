Mingxia Guo works at the intersection of crystallization science, thermodynamics, and process engineering. Her research record spans small molecules, peptides, proteins, and monoclonal antibodies, with emphasis on nucleation mechanisms, polymorphism control, solubility measurement and prediction, peptide conformation, and continuous manufacturing.

Her profile brings together academic depth and industrial relevance, combining rigorous research on crystallization mechanisms with a strong translational focus on scalable processes for pharmaceutical and biopharmaceutical systems.

About

Mingxia Guo is a scientist whose work combines experiment, molecular insight, and process thinking to understand how composition, solvent environment, molecular flexibility, and operating conditions shape crystallization outcomes. Her publication and conference record shows sustained work on peptide solubility, conformation-dependent nucleation, hydrated crystal structures, templated crystallization, small-molecule polymorphism, and crystallization-enabled biomanufacturing.

During her PhD at Imperial College London, she worked across peptide thermodynamics, nucleation and growth, hydrate and dehydrate mechanisms, the role of water in peptide crystallization, peptide conformation, and gSAFT-based thermodynamic prediction. She later continued at Imperial as a Research Associate on amino acid, peptide, and biomolecule crystallization and manufacturing projects supported by Eli Lilly and EPSRC.

At MIT, her work expanded into FDA-funded continuous bio-pharmaceutical crystallization manufacturing for monoclonal antibodies and continuous drug-substance manufacturing from biomass-derived building blocks.
